Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers. Explore our FAQs for quick clarity on common queries

Is hair transplant surgery painful?

No, hair transplant procedures are typically performed under local anesthesia, ensuring you experience minimal to no pain during the surgery itself. Any mild discomfort afterward can usually be managed with prescribed medication.

How long does it take to see results after a hair transplant?

Initial results, such as the shedding of transplanted hair, occur within a few weeks. New hair growth typically begins around 3-4 months, with significant visible growth by 6-9 months, and full results are usually seen within 10-12 months. Patience is key!

Are hair transplant results permanent?

Yes, the transplanted hair follicles are usually taken from areas of your scalp genetically resistant to balding (like the back or sides of your head). Once transplanted, these hairs retain their genetic characteristics and are designed to grow permanently.

What is the difference between FUE and FUT methods?

FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction) involves extracting individual hair follicles directly from the donor area, leaving tiny, nearly invisible dot-like scars. FUT (Follicular Unit Transplantation) involves removing a small strip of scalp from the donor area, from which follicles are then harvested. This leaves a linear scar, though it's typically hidden by surrounding hair. The best method for you depends on your specific needs and hair loss pattern.

Am I a suitable candidate for a hair transplant?

Candidacy depends on several factors, including the extent of your hair loss, the quality and density of your donor hair, and your overall health. A detailed consultation with our specialist will determine if you are an ideal candidate and what results you can realistically expect.

What is the recovery process like after a hair transplant?

Recovery is generally quick. You might experience some redness and swelling for a few days. Specific post-operative care instructions will be provided, including how to wash your hair and what activities to avoid initially. Most patients can resume light daily activities within a few days, with full recovery taking a couple of weeks.
